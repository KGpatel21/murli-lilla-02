───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ★★★ MOST IMPORTANT RULE — CENTER THE MAIN SUBJECT ★★★
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The website crops every hero from the CENTER. On a big monitor you see the
  full wide 16:9 image; on a phone the sides get cut off and ONLY the middle
  vertical strip remains. So:

  >> The single most important / meaningful subject of each image MUST sit in the
     DEAD CENTER of the frame. <<

  If the subject is centered, the image stays meaningful at every screen size —
  from a huge desktop down to a narrow phone. If the subject drifts to a side,
  then on mobile the visitor will just see empty wall / sky / floor in the middle
  and the point of the photo is lost. That is exactly what we must avoid.

  Example of the problem to AVOID: a lab photo where the two scientists are on
  the right and the left half is an empty wall → on mobile the crop shows mostly
  empty wall. Instead, put the scientists in the CENTER so the phone crop shows
  them.

  Practical rule: keep the hero subject inside the centre ~50% of the width, well
  away from the far-left and far-right edges. The left/right edges can hold
  secondary background (sky, blur, scenery) that is safe to lose on mobile.

  (Don't worry about leaving empty space for the headline text — the site lays a
  dark gradient over the image for text legibility, so the subject can be bright
  and centered.)
